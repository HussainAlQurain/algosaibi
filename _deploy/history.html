A Shining Page in the History of the Kingdom

Our History

From pearling and farming in Huraimila to establishing one of the Kingdom's most respected business groups in the Eastern Province.

Early 1900s — Humble Beginnings

The Journey of Hamad bin Ahmad Algosaibi

Born in Huraymila, Sheikh Hamad began his journey with limited resources but strong determination. He worked in pearl diving and agriculture, building the foundation of his work ethic and his deep connection to the people of the Eastern Province.

His vision extended far beyond trade — he sought to build institutions that would serve the Kingdom and its people for generations to come, becoming a cornerstone of the Eastern Province's commercial and industrial development.

Milestones

The Beginning

1940

Establishment of the First Business

Sheikh Hamad founded Hamad Algosaibi Co. in the Eastern Province. During this decade, he built strong ties with Saudi Aramco, supplying silver coins for employee salaries and supporting the company’s early operational needs. He also played a key role in supporting the establishment of the Eastern Province Chamber of Commerce in collaboration with Prince Saud bin Jalawi.

Expansion and Diversification

The company witnessed significant growth, expanding into logistics, industrial supply, and automotive sectors. It became a distributor for Ford Motor Company and an agent for Esso. During this period, Sheikh Hamad also established the first Pepsi Cola bottling plant in the Eastern Province, marking a major milestone in the region’s industrial development.

Building a Legacy

Sheikh Hamad contributed to the establishment of the Eastern Province Chamber of Commerce and supported its early operations, working closely with Prince Saud bin Jalawi.

Continuing the Vision

The legacy continues across generations, guided by enduring values of integrity, ambition, and commitment to national progress.
