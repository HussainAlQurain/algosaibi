About Us

A Legacy of Commercial & Industrial Firsts

Commercially, the Company is known to have established the first warehouse dedicated solely to Saudi Aramco — supplying Aramco's first locally purchased order for steel pipes, tugboats, and tires.

Industrially, the Company pioneered a soft-drinks bottling factory in the mid-1950s and co-founded the first electric power station and Saudi cement plant in the Eastern Province of the Kingdom.

Over the decades, the enterprise has grown into successful businesses spanning manufacturing, trading, shipping, real estate, agriculture, travel services, media, and hospitality — with branches throughout the Kingdom.

A Shining Page in the History of the Kingdom

The Beginning

From Huraimila to Jubail

Originally born in Huraimila in Najd, Sheikh Hamad Algosaibi built his early livelihood through pearling and farming before expanding into trade.

Establishment in 1940

In 1940, Sheikh Hamad established Hamad Algosaibi Co. in the Eastern Province, setting the foundation for a lasting business legacy.

Support for Aramco Payroll

He became known for securing the silver coins used to pay Saudi employees who preferred local currency over U.S. dollars.

Eastern Province Chamber of Commerce

He helped establish and support the Eastern Province Chamber of Commerce during the region's early economic expansion.

Dedicated Aramco Warehouse

Sheikh Hamad strengthened ties with Aramco, established a warehouse dedicated to its operations, and became an early supplier of oil pipes and equipment.

Pepsi Cola and AHAB

Sheikh Hamad crowned his achievements by opening the first Pepsi Cola bottling factory in the Eastern Province and later expanded with his sons into Ahmad Hamad Algosaibi & Bros.
