About Us

A Legacy of Commercial and Industrial Firsts

Commercially, the Company is known to have established the first warehouse dedicated solely to Saudi Aramco. The Company also supplied Saudi Aramco's first locally purchased order for steel pipes, tugboats, and tires.

Industrially, the Company is known for being a pioneer in building a soft drinks filling factory in the mid-1950s, and for being a co-founder of the first electric power station and Saudi cement plant in the Eastern Province of the Kingdom.

The passage of years gave rise to other successful business enterprises in manufacturing, trading, shipping, real estate, agriculture, travel services, media, and hospitality, with branches throughout the Kingdom.

Social and environmental responsibilities have also remained central to the Company's strategy, reflected in its support for community welfare societies, public beautification efforts, and charitable contributions in Saudi Arabia and abroad.

A Shining Page in the History of the Kingdom

The Beginning

From Huraimila to Jubail

Originally born in Huraimila in Najd, Sheikh Hamad Algosaibi built his early livelihood through pearling and farming before expanding into trade.

Establishment in 1940

In 1940, Sheikh Hamad established Hamad Algosaibi Co. in the Eastern Province, setting the foundation for a lasting business legacy.

Support for Aramco Payroll

He became known for securing the silver coins used to pay Saudi employees who preferred local currency over U.S. dollars.

Eastern Province Chamber of Commerce

He helped establish and support the Eastern Province Chamber of Commerce during the region's early economic expansion.

Dedicated Aramco Warehouse

Sheikh Hamad strengthened ties with Aramco, established a warehouse dedicated to its operations, and became an early supplier of oil pipes and equipment.

Expansion into Automotive and Fuel

The Company later supplied tires and boats, opened a fuel station, and became a distributor for Ford, Esso Lubricants, and Atlas Tires.

Pepsi Cola and AHAB

Sheikh Hamad crowned his achievements by opening the first Pepsi Cola bottling factory in the Eastern Province and later expanded the family enterprise with his sons into Ahmad Hamad Algosaibi & Bros.
